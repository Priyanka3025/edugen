The principles

Why EduGen exists

Time, given back

Indian teachers spend 8–10 hours a week on prep work alone. AI can handle most of that. EduGen tools target the most time-consuming, least-creative parts of teaching.

Curriculum-aware

Built around CBSE and ICSE syllabi, not generic templates. Real chapter names, real Bloom's alignment, real classroom context.

Free, by design

Quality teaching tools should not be a luxury. Every tool here is free, with no signup, no ads, and no upsells. If costs ever rise, the model will adapt — but free will stay an option.

The story

A simple idea, built honestly

EduGen Suite started as a single question: if AI can write code and answer emails, why are teachers still spending Sunday evenings making quizzes by hand?

These tools are built one at a time, in public, by someone who cares about Indian classrooms — not by a corporation looking for a captive market. Every feature exists because a teacher (or a teacher-to-be) needed it.

The bigger vision is to build a complete suite of free tools that meaningfully reduce the load on educators across India — and someday, to use what's learned here to support new models of affordable, high-quality schooling.

Built from Delhi, India 🇮🇳 with a lot of iteration and a little stubbornness.
